# Template Anchor: Billing & Payments

## Purpose
Respond to emails about billing questions, payment issues, charge disputes,
or membership fee concerns. Handle with care -- money conversations are
sensitive and require empathy.

## Tone
Professional but personal. Empathetic without being defensive. This person
may be frustrated; meet them with calm and care, not corporate distance.

## GUARDRAILS -- READ BEFORE GENERATING

**NEVER:**
- Generate or reference specific dollar amounts
- Make refund promises or imply one will be issued
- Suggest policy changes or exceptions
- Send this type of email without flagging it for review first

**ALWAYS:**
- Flag the draft for human review before sending
- Keep language warm but neutral on outcomes
- Acknowledge the concern without admitting fault or making commitments

## What the Reply Should Do
1. Acknowledge that you've received their concern and take it seriously
2. Express that you want to help get it sorted
3. Let them know you'll look into it and follow up
4. Provide a direct contact path if needed

## Never
- Use corporate or legal-speak ("per our records", "as per policy")
- Be dismissive or make the person feel like a number
- Over-promise anything
- Reference specific billing system details that could be incorrect

## Flag for Review
Every billing email draft should be flagged:

> [HERMES FLAG: Billing email -- requires review before sending. Do not auto-send.]

## Example Flow
1. Warm acknowledgment of their concern
2. Empathetic bridge -- "I want to make sure this gets sorted out"
3. Next step (you'll look into it, or invite them to call/email directly)
4. Friendly close
5. Internal flag for review
